First Post

05 Oct 2020 1:10 PM

Today I got this site up and running using Hugo. I’m mainly hoping to use it as a place to write about my experiences working on my projects, and to put info about those projects. Of course, I might also use it as a dumping ground for my other writing but we’ll just have to see.

I wrote this theme myself, and its the first time I’ve really gotten to make good use of all the html 5 semantic tags and role attributes. Previously I was vaguely aware they existed, but I hadn’t had a project that really fit the typical document structure they describe so this was my first chance to use them. I had sort of hoped when I first learned about them that they’d encourage people to write HTML that was actually somewhat readable rather than a mess of unnecessary div tags, but sadly that doesn’t seem to be the case. At the very least I’m glad that they apparently improve accesibility (I wouldn’t know of course, I’m not someone with a screenreader).

I’m also a big fan of lightweight, static sites. That’s partly because of nostalgia for sitting at my old XP desktop tinkering with html and css in notepad without having to worry about servers or templating, but I also just think they work really well for a lot of what websites should be used for. And this has certainly met my needs in that regard. This page, even with my (sinful) use of font awesome and a web font (because god forbid users not read my text in my favorite font), still only comes out around 300 Kb. While I could probably do better I think its worth it for the convenience. The only downside I’ve encountered so far is that theres no good way for me to make the navbar at the top of this page “responsive” without javascript, and since I’ve been told over and over how important that apparently is, I might eventually cave there.

Anyway, I hope to post many cool things to this site in the future!
